Learning HTML

By: Queen de los Reyes

HTML, or HyperText Markup Language, stands as the cornerstone of web development, serving as the backbone for creating and structuring content on the World Wide Web. In its essence, HTML is a markup language that utilizes tags to define the structure of a web page. The introduction of an HTML document begins with the 'html' (put <> at the beginning and the end of every tag) tag, followed by the 'head' section, where meta-information, such as the title and links to external resources, is placed. The 'body' tag encapsulates the content visible to users, and within it, various tags like 'h1' to 'h6 '' define headers, 'p' represents paragraphs, and 'a' establishes hyperlinks. Additionally, for videos. The use of attributes within tags further customizes elements, allowing for styling and for creating engaging and interactive digital experiences. In conclusion, mastering the basics of HTML lays the groundwork for effective web design and development, enabling creators to craft seamless and visually appealing online content.
